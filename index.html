About STARTUP

Understanding your Systematic Withdrawal Plan simulator

How It Works

You define your initial capital, and the platform simulates daily returns using simple interest on your original principal. Every day earns the same fixed percentage of your starting investment, giving you a clear, predictable earnings projection. Withdrawals are deducted automatically at the end of each cycle based on your selected frequency. The calculator always projects pure profit — no automatic losses are applied in the simulation.

Investment Parameters

  • Initial Investment — Your starting principal. All daily profits are calculated as a fixed percentage of this base amount.
  • Withdrawal Amount — The fixed sum deducted at the end of each withdrawal cycle.
  • Withdrawal Frequency — Automatically determined: Days/Weeks = Daily, Months = Weekly, Years = Monthly.
  • Expected Return — Daily percentage return on principal. Adjustable from 0% to 30%.
  • Holding Period — Total investment duration in Days, Weeks, Months, or Years. Sundays excluded for month/year calculations.

Profit Structure

Daily Profit = Principal × (Expected Return ÷ 100)

Example: ₹10,000 at 12% daily return generates ₹1,200 per day. Profits are credited daily; withdrawals occur per your frequency setting.

Loss Handling

The platform does not auto-apply losses in the calculator — every investment shows projected profit. In the event of a total loss (admin-triggered), the remaining balance is returned to the user. If the user decides to reinvest, the simulation continues from the remaining amount. Example: ₹500 invested at 12% with a 6% loss event would return ₹470 to the user for potential reinvestment.
